Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between Judo and other martial arts?

Judo focuses primarily on throws, pins, and submissions rather than striking techniques like punches or kicks. It is designed to use the opponent's energy and balance against them. Unlike some other martial arts, Judo also emphasizes respect, discipline, and mutual welfare.

Do I need prior experience in other martial arts to start Judo?

No, you don’t need any prior experience. Judo is suitable for beginners, and classes are structured to accommodate people with no prior martial arts experience. You’ll start by learning basic techniques and build up your skills progressively.

Is Judo safe for beginners?

Yes, Judo is generally considered safe, especially when practiced with proper technique and under the supervision of qualified instructors. Judo focuses on safety and respect for your training partners. The goal is to learn how to fall safely (known as breakfalling) to minimize the risk of injury.

What is the etiquette in Judo training?

Judo emphasizes respect for your training partners, instructors, and the dojo. Some key points of etiquette include:

Bowing when entering or leaving the mat.

Bowing to your opponent before and after sparring.

Being respectful and encouraging to fellow students.

Listening attentively to your instructor.

What is a typical Judo class like for beginners?

A typical Judo class for beginners usually starts with a warm-up, followed by instruction on fundamental techniques, such as basic throws, grips, and falls. You’ll often practice in pairs, with more experienced students helping beginners. The class may end with randori (sparring) or a cool-down session, depending on the dojo’s style and curriculum.

What color uniform can I wear for Judo?

A white or blue gi are the standard gi colors approved for Judo training.

What's the difference between a Judo gi and a BJJ gi?

A Judo gi is made of tougher material that will not rip or tear during prolonged gripping training. BJJ gis are usually made of a lighter material that may tear when exposed to prolonged gripping.

Will I get hurt in Judo?

Like any contact sport, there is a risk of injury. Judo takes steps to reduce injuries by teaching proper breakfall technique (Ukemi).
